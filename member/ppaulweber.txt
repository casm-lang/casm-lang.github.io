== About ==

Philipp Paulweber is a PhD student and university assistant (teaching and research, prae-doc) at the University of Vienna (Universit√§t Wien),
holding a Master of Science (MSc, in German: Dipl-Ing.) degree in computer engineering from the Vienna University of Technology (TU Wien) since 2014.

He was formerly employed at the real-time communication and safety-critical
software/hardware engineering company Time-Triggered Technology (TTTech) located in Vienna
and was involved in automotive software system architecture design and development
applying standards like the ISO 26262 and AUTOSAR.

In 2012 started his ASM theory interest which was hand in hand with the elaboration of his master thesis (in German: Diplomarbeit) with the title "//[[resource:paulweber2014msc:thesis|An optimizing Compiler for the Abstract State Machine Language CASM]]//". All major results of this master thesis are published at the LCTES'14 conference in a paper with the title "//[[resource:lezuo2014lctes:paper|CASM - Optimized Compilation of Abstract State Machines]]//". Moreover, his thesis was nominated for the "//[[resource:paulweber2015epilog:talk|Distinguished Young Alumni Award]]//" in 2015.
